About

A little more about me.

I'm Lea. I grew up in the Chicago suburbs and I've lived here my whole life. I started college at College of DuPage, where I earned my Associate of Arts in May 2024, that's really where I figured out how to be a college student: how to study, how to ask for help, how to actually show up. I transferred to Benedictine University to finish my bachelor's degree, and I'm on track to graduate in December 2026.

After that, I'm planning to start an MBA in Spring 2027. I'm also a registered candidate with the Society of Actuaries, currently studying for Exam FM. Math has always been the subject I trust the most, so it made sense to point it toward something concrete.

Until recently, I worked as a Front Desk Receptionist in the President's Office at Benedictine, which meant most of my days were spent doing detail-heavy, people-facing work that doesn't show up on a transcript but has probably shaped how I think as much as any class has.

On campus

I'm an Executive Board Member of the Global Studies Club, a Senator on Student Senate (Fundraising and Events Committee), and an active member of Women in STEM and the Computer Science Club. I've also worked as an Orientation Leader at both Benedictine and College of DuPage, and I volunteer with the Northern Illinois Food Bank when I can.

Outside of school

None of this is on a transcript, but it's most of what actually takes up space in my head. I got into anime young and never really stopped, it's part of what pulled me toward studying Japanese culture more seriously, history and all, not just the media. Vocaloid is a pretty specific niche of mine, and I love the production side of it as much as the songs; there's something about building a complete song out of synthesized parts that isn't that far off from building software. I'm also drawn to history in general, less about memorizing dates and more about how systems and institutions actually formed, and to philosophy and sociology, mostly the questions about how people organize themselves and why, which pairs better with computer science and math than people expect.
